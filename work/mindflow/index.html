Client work

I led brand identity and content strategy for MindFlow!, a behavioral health and wellness platform — building out their visual system and running the day-to-day content and social strategy that grew their audience and engagement. Below is a look at what I built, organized by type.

MindFlow!'s visual identity is built around one constraint: nothing in the product should feel clinical. The system uses warm paper tones and deep purple as its foundation — a palette that reads as intimate rather than medical — paired with Newsreader italic for all display text, which gives every headline the feeling of a handwritten note rather than a notification.
