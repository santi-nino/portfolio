BFF

Brooklyn Film Festival

Client work

I joined the Brooklyn Film Festival team as a programming assistant for the narrative feature program — one of the largest competitive film festivals in the US. My job was to make the space work for both filmmakers and audiences: coordinating VIP talent, running screening logistics, and hosting the post-film Q&As that turned a screening into a conversation.

The work spanned two sides of the same moment: the logistical work that happens before the lights go down, and the live performance that happens after they come back up. Keeping a film festival on schedule while making it feel effortless is its own discipline — and the Q&A format, where you're moderating an unscripted conversation between filmmakers and a live audience minutes after they watched their own film projected, requires a different kind of presence than anything that happens on a stage.

The skills it built — reading a room, briefing talent under time pressure, keeping energy alive when a conversation stalls — transfer directly into every content and brand role I've held since.

MC & Q&A Moderation — Hosted post-screening Q&As for the narrative feature program, introducing filmmakers, framing the conversation for a live audience, and moderating discussion between directors, writers, and the crowd. Every screening was a different format and a different energy level.

VIP Talent Coordination — Served as first point of contact for filmmakers and talent arriving for their screenings. Managed logistics, briefed them on format, and made sure they felt prepared to step in front of an audience right after watching their own work projected on a full-size screen.

Screening & Event Operations — Coordinated the operational side of each screening: theater setup, run-of-show timing, audience management, and the handoff between film and conversation. When something breaks in a live format, you fix it without the audience noticing.

Post-screening Q&As with narrative feature filmmakers — behind the mic, in the room.
